## High-Speed Blender for Soups and Nut Butter Buying Guide

### Motor Torque, Not Just Wattage

Wattage alone doesn't predict how well a blender handles thick ingredients, since sustained torque under load matters more than peak power on paper. Look for blenders marketed specifically for continuous heavy-duty use rather than short smoothie bursts, since that's the spec that actually determines whether nut butter finishes smooth or stalls halfway.

### Jar Shape and Whether You Need a Tamper

Narrow jars often need a tamper to push thick ingredients back into the blades, while wide-base jars are designed to keep ingredients circulating on their own. If you'd rather not stop and stir mid-batch, a wide-jar model like the Designer 675 or Classic 575 saves that extra step.

### Built-In Heating Versus Friction Heating

Some blenders heat soup purely through blade friction over several minutes, while others include an actual heating element that cooks the ingredients. Friction heating keeps things simpler with one less part to maintain, but a built-in heating element gets soup fully hot faster and more reliably.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can a regular blender make nut butter, or do I need a high-speed model?**
A standard blender can technically get there, but it often stalls or overheats on dense roasted nuts, leaving a chunky result. A high-torque blender for hot soup and nut butter is built to keep pushing through that resistance without straining the motor.

**Does blending soup in a high-speed blender actually make it hot?**
Yes, with enough blending time the friction from a powerful motor can bring cold or lukewarm ingredients up to serving temperature, though models with a built-in heating element get there faster and more consistently.

**How long does it typically take to make nut butter in a high-speed blender?**
Most high-torque blenders take several minutes of continuous blending, often with pauses to scrape down the sides, before roasted nuts break down from crumbly to fully smooth and pourable.

**Is a tamper necessary for making nut butter?**
It's not strictly required if you have a wide-base jar that keeps ingredients circulating, but for narrower jars a tamper makes a real difference in getting every batch to blend evenly instead of leaving unblended pockets.

**Will a high-speed blender damage the jar from hot soup ingredients?**
Not if the jar is rated for hot use, which all the models in this guide are. Standard glass or lower-grade plastic jars not rated for heat are the ones at risk of cracking or warping.
