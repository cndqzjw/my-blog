PETLIBRO's Granary Camera model is the clear choice if visual confirmation matters to you, while WOPET's lineup in this comparison skips cameras entirely in favor of lower prices and larger hoppers. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which automatic feeder has better battery backup?**

The PETLIBRO Air Smart Feeder stands out specifically for its dual battery backup design, which uses two independent compartments rather than the single backup battery slot found on most WOPET models and PETLIBRO's own Granary Camera Feeder. If outage protection is your top priority, that dual-backup design is the more reliable choice.

**Is PETLIBRO or WOPET more reliable long-term?**

Both brands report generally similar reliability for core dispensing function, with most reported issues centering on app connectivity rather than the physical dispensing mechanism failing. PETLIBRO's app has more frequent update cycles reported by users, while WOPET's simpler non-app models avoid the issue entirely by not depending on an app at all.

**Do I need a camera on my automatic feeder?**

A camera matters most if you're monitoring an elderly, sick, or newly adopted pet where confirming actual eating behavior, not just food dispensing, is medically or behaviorally important. For a healthy adult pet with a consistent appetite, a non-camera model like the WOPET 6L or WOPET 10-Cup covers the core scheduling need at a lower price.

**Can these feeders handle wet food?**

Standard dry food feeders from both PETLIBRO and WOPET are not designed for wet food, since it spoils quickly without refrigeration; the PETLIBRO Polar Wet Food Feeder is the specific model built to solve this with an active cooling compartment. If you're feeding wet food on any kind of schedule, a standard dry hopper feeder is the wrong tool regardless of brand.

**How do these compare to a manual timed feeder without an app?**

An app-connected feeder like the PETLIBRO Granary or WOPET 6L adds remote schedule changes and, in PETLIBRO's case, live camera viewing, while a no-app unit like the WOPET 10-Cup only allows on-site adjustments.
