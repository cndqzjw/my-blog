## Chew-Resistant GPS Collar Buying Guide

If you're specifically shopping for a durable GPS collar for chewers, the decision comes down to three factors: whether your dog is chewing the tracker itself or the collar band it hangs from, whether physical redesign or behavioral deterrent will address the actual cause, and how much you're willing to change your current setup versus adding a targeted fix.

### Built-in versus clip-on tracker design

A dog that specifically targets a dangling clip-on unit is better served by an integrated design like the Fi Series 3, which removes the physical target entirely, while a dog that chews more out of general curiosity about anything new may do fine with a reinforced clip-on option like the Tractive GPS Dog XL.

### Collar material matters as much as the tracker

If the chewing is directed at the collar band itself rather than the tracker housing, switching to a biothane collar like the Viper Tactical model changes what the dog's teeth can actually grip onto, independent of which tracker brand you use.

### Hardware fixes work best paired with behavioral tools

A reinforced housing reduces damage from any single chewing episode, but a bitter-taste deterrent like Grannick's Bitter Apple Spray addresses the behavior itself, and using both together is more effective than relying on tougher hardware alone to outlast a determined chewer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best collar attachment for GPS trackers on a dog that chews?**

A secure, locking clip mechanism like the one on the Tractive GPS Dog XL, paired with a chew-resistant biothane collar like the Viper Tactical Collar, gives the tracker less to grip onto and a sturdier housing if the dog does reach it. **Why does my dog keep chewing its GPS tracker?**

Dogs often chew at a new collar attachment simply because it's an unfamiliar object within reach, though persistent or escalating chewing can also indicate anxiety, teething in younger dogs, or skin irritation under the collar. Behavior-monitoring trackers like the Whistle Go Explore can help you spot whether the chewing correlates with other behavior changes worth mentioning to a vet.

**Can I train my dog to stop chewing its collar?**

Yes, pairing a bitter-taste deterrent like Grannick's Bitter Apple Spray with consistent redirection when you catch the behavior starting can reduce chewing over time, though some dogs respond better to a physical hardware change than to taste deterrents alone. A reinforced housing like the Tractive GPS Dog XL reduces how much damage a single chewing episode causes and can outlast lighter-duty trackers, but it does not stop the behavior itself, a determined chewer can still eventually work through even a rugged casing with enough repeated attempts.
