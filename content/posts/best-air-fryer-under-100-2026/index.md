## Air Fryer Buying Guide

If you are shopping for the best air fryer under $100, the decision usually comes down to four practical factors: how much capacity your household actually needs, how much noise and counter space you can tolerate, how easy the parts are to clean, and whether even heating holds up across a full basket.

### Capacity and wattage

Capacity should match how many people you are actually cooking for, not how many you occasionally host. A 2.6 to 4-quart basket is realistic for one or two people eating single portions. If you are shopping for the best air fryer for family of 4, look at 5.8 quarts or larger so a full meal fits in one layer instead of requiring two cooking rounds.

Wattage in the 1500W to 1700W range is typical for this price bracket and generally correlates with faster preheating and more consistent crisping, though basket shape matters just as much as raw wattage.

### Cleaning and materials

An air fryer that is annoying to clean gets used less over time, no matter how well it cooks. Prioritize a dishwasher-safe basket and crisper tray, and confirm the nonstick coating is BPA-free and PFOA-free. Baskets with a nonstick coating that starts flaking are the most common long-term complaint, so a reputable coating matters more than it seems at purchase time.

### Noise level and even heating

Noise is an underrated factor, especially in an open-concept kitchen or a small apartment where the air fryer runs during a video call or while a baby is sleeping. Most models in this range run at a moderate hum, but heavily used units with worn fan bearings can get noticeably louder over a year of daily cycles.

Even heating shows up most clearly when you cook a full basket rather than a half-empty one. Square or wider baskets tend to distribute hot air more evenly than tall, narrow buckets, which is why basket shape is worth checking alongside capacity.

### Counter footprint

A 5.8-quart air fryer takes up meaningfully more space than a 2.6-quart model, and that difference matters in a kitchen that already holds a coffee maker, toaster, and dish rack. If storage between uses is not realistic, measure your available counter space before choosing based on capacity alone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does an air fryer last?

Most air fryers in this price range last around 3 to 5 years with regular use, assuming the basket and heating element are treated with reasonable care. Avoiding metal utensils that scratch the nonstick coating and cleaning after each use both meaningfully extend that lifespan.

### Is an air fryer healthier than frying?

Yes, in most everyday cases. An air fryer typically uses a tablespoon or less of oil, compared to a pot of oil in deep frying, which meaningfully reduces overall fat content while still producing a crisp exterior through hot air circulation. It will not replicate deep-fried texture exactly, but for weeknight cooking it is a genuinely healthier method, not just a marketing claim.

### What size air fryer do I need for a family of 4?

For a family of 4, a 5.8-quart or larger basket is usually the safest choice. That size lets you cook a full meal, like chicken pieces and a vegetable side, in a single layer instead of running the machine twice, which also saves time on busy weeknights.

### How do I clean an air fryer?

Let the basket cool, then wash the basket and crisper tray with warm soapy water or run them through the dishwasher if they are rated dishwasher-safe. Wipe the interior heating chamber with a damp cloth to remove grease splatter, and avoid submerging the main unit itself in water since it houses the heating element and fan.

### What's the difference between an air fryer and a convection oven?

An air fryer is essentially a compact, high-speed convection oven with a smaller chamber, which lets it preheat faster and circulate hot air more intensely around food. A full-sized convection oven has more capacity for large batches or bigger cuts of meat, but an air fryer usually wins on speed and energy use for single meals and weeknight portions.
