## Mechanical Keyboard Buying Guide

When you choose a keyboard under 100, your decision usually comes down to three things: how you type, how much space you have, and how much noise you can tolerate.

### Switch type and feel

The switch is the heart of a mechanical keyboard. Linear switches feel smooth and fast, tactile switches provide a noticeable bump, and clicky switches deliver more feedback. For typing, tactile switches are often easier to live with. For gaming, linear switches can feel quicker and less distracting. If you are not sure, a mid-range tactile board is a safe everyday choice.

### Layout size and desk footprint

A full-size keyboard adds a number pad and more room, but it also eats up more desk space. A 65% or 60% board is better for smaller setups and can make your workspace feel cleaner. The right choice depends on how often you use arrow keys, function shortcuts, and number keys.

### Noise level and office suitability

Mechanical keyboards can be loud, especially with clicky switches. If you use the keyboard in a shared home office or apartment, a quieter tactile board is usually a better fit. The best quiet mechanical keyboard is not necessarily the one with the lowest profile; it is the one that gives you a satisfying feel without sounding like a typewriter during a call.

### Build quality and durability

A keyboard should feel solid when you type, not flimsy in the corners or loose around the frame. Keycaps, stabilizers, and switch mounts all matter. If the board has a more durable case and nicer keycaps, it usually feels better after weeks of repeated use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are mechanical keyboards good for typing?

Yes, especially if you spend long hours writing, coding, or working in spreadsheets. Mechanical switches often make typing feel more precise and less fatiguing than rubber dome alternatives.

### Is a 60% keyboard worth it?

It can be, if you value desk space and do not rely heavily on the number pad or function keys. For minimal setups and compact workstations, the tradeoff is usually worth it.

### What is the difference between linear and tactile switches?

Linear switches feel smoother and more direct, while tactile switches provide a noticeable bump that helps confirm a key press. Many people prefer tactile switches for typing and linear switches for gaming.

### Are mechanical keyboards noisy?

They can be, especially with clicky switches. If you want something quieter, look for tactile or silent switches and a board with strong sound dampening.

### Which mechanical keyboard is best for office use?

A compact board with quiet switches and a stable layout is usually the best office choice. The Keychron K3 and the HyperX Alloy Origins 60 are strong examples if you want a cleaner desk setup without losing the mechanical typing feel.
