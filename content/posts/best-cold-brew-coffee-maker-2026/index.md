## Cold Brew Coffee Maker Buying Guide

Choosing between these comes down to how much concentrate you actually go through in a week and how much fridge space you're willing to give up, more than any single feature.

### Match capacity to how often you'll refill

A 1-quart pitcher works fine if you're the only one drinking cold brew and don't mind steeping every three to four days, but a household of two or three daily drinkers will empty that same pitcher in a day or two, making a larger tap-style carafe like the Mr. Coffee a better long-term fit.

### Check how long to steep cold brew coffee for your setup

How long to steep cold brew coffee depends on grind size and concentrate strength, but 12 to 24 hours at room temperature or in the fridge is the standard range across nearly all of these makers, with 16–18 hours producing a balanced result for most coarse grinds. Steeping longer than 24 hours starts pulling more bitter compounds without meaningfully improving strength.

### Decide between fridge-door and countertop steeping

If your fridge is already tight on space, a countertop steeper like the OXO avoids that problem entirely, while a narrow pitcher like the Hario Mizudashi is built specifically to slot into a fridge door shelf without hogging room other groceries need.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the best cold brew coffee maker for beginners?**

The Takeya Cold Brew Coffee Maker is the easiest starting point because the ratio, filtration, and cleanup are all straightforward with no learning curve, and its airtight seal forgives minor mistakes in steep time better than more exposed setups.

**How long to steep cold brew coffee for the smoothest taste?**

Most coarse-ground cold brew reaches its best balance of smoothness and strength between 16 and 18 hours; shorter steeps taste thin and under-extracted, while steeps well past 24 hours start tasting flat and slightly bitter rather than stronger.

**Can I use a cold brew coffee maker for concentrate to make hot coffee too?**

Yes, cold brew concentrate can be diluted with hot water instead of cold or milk for a quick hot cup, though the flavor profile stays naturally lower in acidity and different from coffee brewed hot to begin with, since the extraction process itself is what changes, not just the temperature at serving.

**Do I need special coarse-ground coffee for cold brew, or will any grind work?**

A coarse grind, similar to what you'd use for a French press, is strongly recommended, since fine grounds slip through most mesh filters and over-extract during the long steep, producing a muddier, more bitter concentrate than a properly coarse grind.

**How much coffee concentrate does one batch typically make?**

That depends entirely on the maker's capacity, ranging from roughly 3 cups on the compact OXO system up to about 25oz of concentrate on the Mr. Coffee, which stretches to closer to 50oz of finished drink once diluted with water, milk, or ice.
